# Impact and Evaluation Framework  
Fundacja BBS — Better Balance System

## 1. Purpose

This document outlines the intended areas of impact and evaluation criteria associated with the research and development activities of Fundacja BBS — Better Balance System.

The Foundation operates as a research-oriented entity. The impacts described below represent intended contribution areas rather than guaranteed outcomes.

---

## 2. Institutional Impact Areas

### 2.1 Governance Transparency

Research efforts aim to:

- improve documentation clarity,
- strengthen traceability of analytical processes,
- support structured audit workflows.

Expected contribution: enhanced procedural transparency within pilot or research environments.

---

### 2.2 Decision Support Quality

Analytical tools are designed to:

- improve consistency of structured reasoning,
- support risk-aware evaluation,
- document assumptions and analytical pathways.

Expected contribution: better-informed human decision-making.

---

### 2.3 Administrative Efficiency (Research Context)

Digital modeling and process structuring may help:

- identify inefficiencies,
- reduce duplication of documentation,
- streamline pilot workflows.

Efficiency gains depend on institutional context and implementation scope.

---

### 2.4 AI Governance and Safety

The Foundation’s AI research contributes to:

- risk-based AI classification,
- human oversight integration,
- procedural bias mitigation,
- documentation-driven accountability.

Impact is evaluated through compliance alignment and review procedures.

---

### 2.5 Economic and Operational Considerations

Structured digital processes may support:

- improved documentation management,
- reduction of preventable process errors,
- enhanced clarity in financial and procedural records.

Quantifiable impact depends on specific implementation environments.

---

## 3. Evaluation Criteria

Impact is assessed using:

- documentation completeness,
- compliance with regulatory frameworks,
- audit traceability,
- human oversight integration,
- stakeholder feedback during pilot projects.

No absolute outcomes are assumed.

---

## 4. Regulatory Alignment

All impact activities are conducted in alignment with:

- GDPR,
- the EU AI Act,
- Polish foundation and accounting law,
- applicable public-sector innovation standards.

---

## 5. Conclusion

Fundacja BBS — Better Balance System aims to contribute to structured, transparent, and responsible digital governance research.

Impact is evaluated proportionately, based on real-world testing, regulatory alignment, and institutional collaboration.
