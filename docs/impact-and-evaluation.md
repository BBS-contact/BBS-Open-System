# Impact and Evaluation Framework  
Fundacja BBS — Better Balance System

## 1. Purpose

This document defines the impact areas, evaluation methodology, and European regulatory alignment of Fundacja BBS — Better Balance System.

The Foundation operates as a Polish non-profit research entity. All impact statements describe intended contribution areas and do not constitute guarantees of systemic outcomes.

---

## 2. Institutional Impact Areas

### 2.1 Governance Transparency

Research efforts aim to:

- improve documentation clarity,
- strengthen traceability of analytical processes,
- support structured audit workflows.

Expected contribution: enhanced procedural transparency within pilot and research environments.

---

### 2.2 Decision Support Quality

Analytical systems are designed to:

- improve structured reasoning consistency,
- support risk-aware evaluation,
- document analytical assumptions and pathways.

Expected contribution: better-informed human decision-making.

---

### 2.3 Administrative Efficiency (Research Context)

Digital modeling and workflow structuring may help:

- identify process inefficiencies,
- reduce duplication of documentation,
- streamline structured pilot workflows.

Efficiency gains depend on institutional context and implementation scope.

---

### 2.4 AI Governance and Safety

The Foundation’s AI-related research contributes to:

- risk-based AI classification,
- documentation of analytical outputs,
- integration of human oversight,
- procedural bias mitigation strategies.

Impact is evaluated through compliance alignment and structured review procedures.

---

### 2.5 Operational and Organizational Integrity

Structured digital processes may support:

- improved documentation management,
- reduction of preventable process errors,
- enhanced clarity in financial and governance records.

Quantifiable outcomes depend on pilot scope and institutional cooperation.

---

## 3. Evaluation Criteria

Impact is assessed using:

- documentation completeness,
- audit traceability,
- compliance with regulatory frameworks,
- human oversight integration,
- stakeholder feedback in pilot projects.

No absolute systemic outcomes are assumed.

---

## 4. EU Strategic Alignment

The Foundation’s research activities align with selected European priorities in:

- digital transformation of governance processes,
- AI risk management frameworks,
- transparency-enhancing digital architecture,
- public-interest technology development.

Alignment references include:

- Regulation (EU) 2016/679 (GDPR),
- the EU Artificial Intelligence Act,
- European Commission guidance on trustworthy AI,
- OECD AI Principles.

The Foundation does not claim institutional mandate but seeks constructive regulatory alignment.

---

## 5. Regulatory Context

All activities are conducted under:

- Polish foundation law,
- EU regulatory frameworks,
- documented compliance procedures,
- internal governance oversight.

Impact evaluation remains proportional and context-dependent.

---

## 6. Conclusion

Fundacja BBS — Better Balance System aims to contribute to responsible digital governance research through structured transparency, AI risk awareness, and compliance-oriented system design.

Impact is measured through real-world pilot testing, regulatory alignment, and institutional cooperation.
