# Ethical Governance Framework  
Fundacja BBS — Better Balance System

## 1. Purpose

This document outlines the ethical governance principles guiding the activities of Fundacja BBS — Better Balance System.

The Foundation operates as a research-oriented and governance-focused institution under Polish law. Ethical standards inform its technological development, analytical systems, and institutional operations.

---

## 2. Ethical Foundation

The Foundation’s ethical framework is grounded in:

- respect for human dignity,
- non-discrimination,
- proportionality,
- transparency,
- accountability,
- rule of law,
- public interest orientation.

These principles guide internal decision-making and system design.

---

## 3. Ethical Technology Development

In the development and evaluation of digital systems, including AI-based analytical tools:

- systems are designed to support, not replace, human decision-making;
- manipulation, coercion, or hidden influence are not permitted;
- bias mitigation and fairness considerations are integrated into evaluation processes;
- transparency is applied proportionately, balancing openness and security.

Ethical safeguards are procedural and governance-based rather than declarative guarantees.

---

## 4. Human Oversight

Human responsibility remains central to all operations.

AI systems and analytical tools operate in an advisory capacity. Final accountability rests with designated human authorities within the Foundation.

---

## 5. Transparency and Documentation

The Foundation maintains structured documentation regarding:

- system design,
- governance processes,
- compliance mechanisms,
- risk management procedures.

Public transparency is implemented in accordance with legal obligations and intellectual property protection.

---

## 6. Risk Awareness

The Foundation recognizes that digital technologies can create risks, including:

- unintended bias,
- misuse of outputs,
- overreliance on automated analysis,
- data protection concerns.

Ethical governance includes continuous risk assessment and procedural mitigation strategies.

---

## 7. Institutional Commitment

The Foundation commits to:

- lawful and responsible operation,
- respect for fundamental rights,
- alignment with European regulatory standards,
- ongoing review and improvement of ethical safeguards.

Ethical governance evolves in response to regulatory, societal, and technological developments.

---

## 8. Regulatory Context

This framework aligns with:

- EU fundamental rights principles,
- GDPR,
- the EU AI Act,
- Polish national legislation.

Binding authority remains with applicable law.
