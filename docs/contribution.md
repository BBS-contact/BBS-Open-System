# Contribution Guide — How to Participate in the BBS Ecosystem

This document outlines how individuals, institutions, and contributors
can safely and ethically participate in the development of BBS.

BBS is an open, human-centered digital ecosystem built on truth,
transparency, and responsibility. Participation follows the same principles.

---

# 1. Participation Principles

### • Ethical contribution  
All contributions must align with ethical standards of truth,
non-harm, transparency, and respect for human dignity.

### • Open collaboration  
BBS welcomes contributors from all backgrounds and fields.

### • Responsibility-based structure  
No contributor receives privileged control or hidden authority.

### • Transparency  
All public contributions remain visible and traceable.

### • Safety  
Sensitive components of BBS remain protected until full maturity.

---

# 2. Who Can Contribute?

- Developers  
- Designers  
- Researchers  
- Data analysts  
- Ethical technologists  
- Auditors  
- Lawyers  
- Educators  
- Medics  
- Civic innovators  
- Volunteers  

Anyone aligned with the mission and ethics of BBS is welcome.

---

# 3. What Contributions Are Accepted?

### ✔ Public-facing modules  
Documentation, interface design, public code layers, and tools.

### ✔ Research and analysis  
Studies, domain expertise, modelling, and validation.

### ✔ Ethical review  
Input from ethicists, legal professionals, and auditors.

### ✔ Community support  
Translations, outreach, communication, educational materials.

### ✔ Testing  
Interface, usability, and systems testing feedback.

---

# 4. What Contributions Are Not Accepted?

### ✘ Manipulative content  
Anything aimed at harm, misinformation, or biased influence.

### ✘ Privileged access requests  
No contributor may request hidden powers or privileged control.

### ✘ Unsafe code  
No unverified modules that can compromise ethical or security layers.

### ✘ Political or ideological content  
BBS remains neutral and non-partisan.

---

# 5. Contribution Process

### 5.1 Discussion  
Contributors may propose ideas publicly.

### 5.2 Review  
All proposals undergo transparency and ethics review.

### 5.3 Approval  
Accepted ideas are added to the roadmap or documentation.

### 5.4 Implementation  
Contributors work on non-sensitive modules or documentation.

Sensitive algorithms and internal layers remain closed until safety maturity.

---

# 6. Code of Conduct

All contributors must follow these rules:

- Operate respectfully and responsibly  
- Do not attempt corruption, exploitation, or manipulation  
- Maintain transparency  
- Respect privacy and rights of others  
- Uphold truth-based dialogue  
- Avoid hostile or harmful behaviour  

Violations result in immediate removal from participation.

---

# 7. How to Start

You can begin by:

- reading the architecture and ethics documents  
- learning the platform modules  
- joining discussions and proposing improvements  
- contributing documentation or non-sensitive code  
- participating in research or ethical review
