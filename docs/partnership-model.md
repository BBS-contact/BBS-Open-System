# Institutional Collaboration Model  
Fundacja BBS — Better Balance System

## 1. Purpose

This document outlines the structured collaboration model of Fundacja BBS — Better Balance System.

The Foundation operates as a Polish non-profit research entity. Collaboration is oriented toward research, pilot evaluation, and compliance-aligned innovation.

---

## 2. Types of Collaboration

### 2.1 Research Partnerships

Collaboration may focus on:

- AI governance and risk assessment,
- transparency-enhancing documentation systems,
- governance process modeling,
- compliance-oriented digital tools,
- interdisciplinary research initiatives.

Research partnerships are governed by written agreements and institutional review procedures.

---

### 2.2 Pilot Research Projects

Where appropriate, limited-scope pilot projects may be conducted in controlled environments such as:

- academic institutions,
- research laboratories,
- non-profit organizations,
- administrative units participating in research evaluation.

Pilot projects are subject to regulatory review and risk assessment.

---

### 2.3 Co-Development Initiatives

The Foundation may engage in structured co-development of:

- research prototypes,
- documentation frameworks,
- educational materials,
- compliance-support tools.

Intellectual property arrangements are defined contractually.

---

## 3. Governance Principles

All partnerships operate under:

- Polish foundation law,
- applicable EU regulations,
- GDPR compliance,
- the Foundation’s internal governance framework.

Collaboration does not grant automatic governance control or system authority beyond formal agreements.

---

## 4. Independence and Boundaries

The Foundation maintains:

- institutional autonomy,
- ethical governance oversight,
- compliance review procedures.

Partnerships are designed to support research and innovation rather than political or commercial objectives.

---

## 5. Engagement Process

Partnership proposals are evaluated based on:

- statutory alignment,
- legal feasibility,
- institutional capacity,
- risk assessment outcomes.

Formal cooperation requires documented agreements.
