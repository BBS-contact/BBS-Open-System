# LEO Project Status

This document describes the current development stage of the **LEO – Logical Epistemic Oversight** research project.

The system is developed by **Fundacja BBS – Better Balance System** within a controlled research environment.

---

# Development Phases

## Phase 1 — Conceptual Architecture

Completed

The initial phase focused on defining the architecture of the LEO system, including:

- epistemic governance model
- integrity verification concepts
- anomaly analysis framework
- structured decision-support architecture

---

## Phase 2 — Prototype Infrastructure

Completed

The current prototype includes the following operational subsystems:

- structured truth ingestion
- anomaly detection engine
- graph analysis engine
- pattern detection
- cluster analysis
- investigation report generation
- risk escalation mechanisms
- institutional alert pipeline

Runtime demonstrations of these components are available in the repository.

---

## Phase 3 — Research Sandbox Testing

Active

The system is currently tested in a controlled research environment operated by the BBS Foundation.

The sandbox environment allows:

- validation of analytical pipelines
- testing anomaly detection scenarios
- simulation of institutional workflows
- structured logging of system outputs

---

## Phase 4 — Structured Pilot Readiness

Planned

The next phase involves preparation for structured institutional pilot programs under appropriate regulatory and institutional supervision.

Potential areas of research collaboration include:

- digital governance infrastructure
- institutional transparency tools
- compliance-aware AI advisory systems
- anomaly detection in administrative and financial processes

---

# Research Objective

The objective of the LEO research initiative is to explore **preventive integrity infrastructure** capable of identifying structural anomalies in institutional processes before systemic failures occur.

---

# Research Scope

LEO is designed as a **research and advisory system**.

It does not replace institutional authority and does not issue legally binding decisions.
