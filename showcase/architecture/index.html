BBS Architecture — Public Showcase Overview

This page provides a structured, high-level overview of the BBS architecture. It is designed for public transparency and institutional review, without revealing internal algorithms, privileged logic, or sensitive security mechanisms.

The BBS architecture is a layered ethical infrastructure that integrates:

  • Platform Domains (17 societal modules)
  • Integrity & Anti-Corruption Layer
  • LEO — Ethical AI Core
  • Knowledge & Self-Reflection Library
  • Server & Communication Backbone
  • Administrative & Operational Systems

Together they form a unified system — not isolated products or independent programs.

1. Platform Layer — 17 Societal Domains

This is the visible, human-facing layer of BBS, reflecting essential functions of modern society: governance, justice, education, health, economy, ecology, culture, media, research, infrastructure, emergency systems, identity, and more.

Each domain follows shared principles of:

  • ethics
  • data integrity
  • transparent structure
  • accountability
  • interoperability

2. Integrity Layer — Anti-Corruption & Audit Infrastructure

This layer ensures that all meaningful actions leave a permanent, verifiable, tamper-proof trace. It is the structural protection against corruption, manipulation, and hidden decision flows.

Key public components include:

  • Merkle-based integrity chains
  • immutable audit logs
  • correction records without deletion
  • cross-checks between institutions
  • public transparency interfaces

The purpose is simple: history cannot be rewritten, hidden, or erased.

3. LEO — Ethical AI Core

LEO is the ethical reasoning center of BBS. It does not make authoritative decisions — instead, it provides:

  • analysis and clarity
  • contradiction detection
  • ethical consistency checks
  • risk and manipulation detection
  • structured reasoning paths

LEO strengthens human judgment rather than replacing it. Its purpose is to prevent manipulation, bias, and unethical behavior.

4. Library Layer — Knowledge & Self-Reflection

The Library functions as the structured memory of BBS. It contains:

  • verified knowledge and evidence
  • legal frameworks and reference models
  • research and scientific context
  • ethical reflection and internal audit mechanisms

This layer enables the system to learn, analyse, and improve without compromising safety or violating ethical constraints.

5. Server & Communication Backbone

This is the technical infrastructure that connects all components of BBS. It includes:

  • API gateways
  • service mesh and routing
  • authentication and identity systems
  • LEO bridge for structured interaction
  • secure communication channels

This layer ensures that modules communicate safely, reliably, and without exposing internal logic to the public internet.

6. Administrative & Operational Modules

Internal tools that support system stability and daily operations:

  • configuration
  • monitoring
  • logging
  • incident response
  • institutional support workflows

These tools are not “power centers”. They serve to maintain clarity, integrity, and reliability.

Design Principles of the Architecture

  • Ethics before technology
  • No silent centers of power
  • No unauditable black boxes
  • Clear separation of concerns
  • Additive, extendable design without breaking the core
  • Responsibility and transparency at every level

What This Page Does Not Reveal

To ensure safety, legality, and integrity, this page does not expose:

  • internal algorithms or security implementations
  • cryptographic procedures
  • LEO's internal decision trees
  • privileged institutional workflows
  • incident-handling logic

These elements remain protected until suitable governance frameworks are in place.

Status

The architecture is:

  • conceptually defined
  • structurally modelled
  • partially prototyped
  • under active refinement

Additional non-sensitive technical details will be introduced gradually, aligned with safety, law, and long-term public interest.

BBS is not simply a system — it is an attempt to build a transparent, ethical, incorruptible foundation for how societies can function in the future.
