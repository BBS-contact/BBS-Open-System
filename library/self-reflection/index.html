Self-Reflection Engine — Internal Audit, Consistency, and Ethical Coherence The Self-Reflection Engine is the inner examiner of BBS and LEO. It is responsible for checking how the system thinks, what conclusions it makes, and whether these conclusions remain consistent with the core ethical and logical principles. Its purpose is not to control users, but to control the internal integrity of the system itself. The Self-Reflection Engine helps ensure that reasoning remains: consistent over time resistant to hidden bias aligned with core ethical principles transparent and traceable open to correction and improvement Objectives • Internal honesty The system must be able to “look at itself” and detect contradictions, gaps, or dangerous drifts in logic. • Bias awareness Instead of pretending to be neutral, the engine actively searches for potential bias sources and flags them. • Ethical coherence Conclusions, recommendations, and risk assessments must remain compatible with the ethical core of BBS. • Continuous improvement Detected issues are not hidden — they are recorded, analysed, and used to improve future decisions. • Traceable reasoning Human reviewers must be able to see how key conclusions were reached and what doubts were identified internally. Core Components (Public Overview) Reasoning Consistency Checker Analyses chains of arguments and decisions for logical conflicts, circular reasoning, or unexplained jumps. Bias & Drift Monitor Monitors patterns over time to detect slow drift away from ethical or logical baselines. Ethical Coherence Validator Checks whether decisions and recommendations remain compatible with BBS core principles and declared policies. Self-Audit Log Keeps an internal record of detected issues, doubts, conflicts, and corrections — with integrity protection. Reflection Interface for Human Review Allows authorized human reviewers to inspect internal doubts, tensions, and flagged risks. Why It Matters Without self-reflection, any complex system can: repeat the same mistakes silently accumulate bias become overconfident and opaque deviate from its declared principles The Self-Reflection Engine provides a different approach: it formalises internal doubt, careful self-checking, and transparent error handling as essential parts of intelligence — not as weaknesses. Development Status The Self-Reflection Engine is conceptually defined and linked to: LEO’s reasoning patterns the Integrity Layer (for protected self-audit logs) future oversight tools for human reviewers Technical implementation of pattern analysis, bias monitoring, and consistency checks will be developed gradually, together with the evolution of LEO and BBS governance mechanisms.
