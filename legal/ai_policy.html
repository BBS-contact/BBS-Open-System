AI Governance Policy

Regulatory alignment, risk-based oversight, and responsible AI development

1. Purpose and Scope

This Policy defines the governance framework for the development, testing, and deployment of artificial intelligence systems within Fundacja BBS — Better Balance System ("Foundation"), including the LEO system.

The Policy applies to research, analytical, and advisory AI systems developed or used in connection with the Foundation’s statutory objectives.

2. Regulatory Alignment

AI-related activities are conducted in alignment with:

  • Regulation (EU) 2016/679 (GDPR);
  • Applicable Polish data protection laws;
  • The EU Artificial Intelligence Act framework (risk-based approach);
  • Relevant sectoral and information security regulations.

The Foundation monitors regulatory developments and adjusts internal procedures accordingly.

3. Risk-Based Classification

AI systems are assessed using a proportional risk-based methodology consistent with the EU AI Act model:

  • Minimal risk systems – documentation and transparency requirements;
  • Limited risk systems – user information and traceability safeguards;
  • Higher-risk systems – enhanced documentation, testing, monitoring, and human oversight mechanisms.

Where required, risk assessments and impact evaluations are formally documented.

4. Human Oversight and Accountability

AI systems operated by the Foundation function as advisory and analytical tools. Final decisions remain under human authority.

  • No fully autonomous legal or administrative decision-making is delegated to AI;
  • Critical outputs are subject to review by designated human operators;
  • Clear responsibility remains with the Management Board and authorized personnel.

5. Data Governance and Security

AI systems operate under the Foundation’s Privacy Policy and internal data governance standards.

  • Data minimization and purpose limitation principles apply;
  • Access controls and integrity safeguards are implemented;
  • Where required, Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIA) are conducted.

6. Transparency and Documentation

The Foundation maintains internal documentation concerning system architecture, versioning, and risk controls.

Public-facing explanations are provided where legally appropriate and without compromising security or intellectual property protections.

7. Prohibited Practices

The Foundation does not develop or deploy AI systems for:

  • Unlawful surveillance or covert profiling;
  • Manipulation of individuals in violation of fundamental rights;
  • Discriminatory or unjustified bias-based decision systems;
  • Activities prohibited under the EU AI Act or applicable law.

8. Review and Updates

This Policy is reviewed periodically and updated where required by regulatory developments or operational changes.

The legally binding framework remains the applicable EU and Polish law.
