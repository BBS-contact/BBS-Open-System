AI Policy

The ethical and transparent use of AI in the BBS ecosystem.

1. Purpose

The BBS AI Policy defines how AI systems (including LEO) are developed, deployed, and governed. It ensures that AI serves humanity, upholds transparency, and protects fundamental rights.

AI systems within BBS are designed and classified according to a risk-based approach, ensuring proportionate safeguards for higher-risk use cases.

2. Core Principles

  • Truth First: AI must prioritize truth over manipulation, profit, or ideology.
  • Human-Centered: AI exists to support people, never to dominate or exploit them.
  • Transparency: All AI decisions must be traceable and explainable.
  • Justice: AI may not discriminate or reinforce systemic bias.
  • Responsibility: AI remains accountable to human oversight at all times.

3. Prohibited Uses

  • No use of AI for coercion, manipulation, or surveillance beyond lawful transparency needs.
  • No use for military aggression, disinformation, or social exploitation.
  • No hidden profiling, tracking, or commercial resale of data.

4. Data and Privacy

AI follows the BBS Privacy Policy. Data is processed minimally, securely, and only for declared purposes. No advertising, no data sale, no hidden tracking.

5. Safety and Oversight

  • Regular independent audits of AI systems.
  • Public transparency reports published through the Change Log.
  • Failsafe mechanisms to prevent unintended harm.
  • Continuous human supervision in critical decisions.

6. Liability

AI does not replace human accountability. Any decision or action influenced by AI must be reviewable by designated human authorities. The responsibility remains with the operators and the organization, not with the system itself.

Individuals affected by AI-assisted decisions have the right to request a human review and a meaningful explanation of such decisions.

7. Updates

This AI Policy will evolve with new knowledge, technology, and societal needs. All updates will be recorded in the Change Log.
