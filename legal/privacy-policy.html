Privacy Policy

Personal data protection and GDPR compliance framework

1. Data Controller

The controller of personal data is Fundacja BBS — Better Balance System, registered in the National Court Register (KRS 0001220296), with its registered office in Brodnica, Poland.

Contact for data protection matters is available via the official communication channels published on the Foundation’s website.

2. Legal Basis for Processing

Personal data is processed in accordance with Regulation (EU) 2016/679 (GDPR) and applicable Polish data protection laws.

Processing is based on one or more of the following legal grounds:

  • Consent of the data subject (Art. 6(1)(a) GDPR);
  • Performance of a contract (Art. 6(1)(b));
  • Compliance with a legal obligation (Art. 6(1)(c));
  • Legitimate interests pursued by the Foundation (Art. 6(1)(f)), provided such interests are not overridden by fundamental rights and freedoms.

3. Categories of Data

The Foundation may process identification data, contact data, professional or academic affiliation data, project participation data, and technical data related to website usage.

Special categories of data are processed only where explicitly required by law or with explicit consent.

4. Purpose of Processing

  • Implementation of statutory objectives and research activities;
  • Grant, partnership, and cooperation management;
  • Volunteer and employment administration;
  • Compliance with legal and accounting obligations;
  • Ensuring information security and system integrity.

5. Data Retention

Personal data is retained only for the period necessary to fulfill the purpose for which it was collected, or as required by applicable law (including accounting and archival obligations).

Retention periods are defined in internal data governance and data retention policies.

6. Data Subject Rights

Data subjects have the right to:

  • Access their personal data;
  • Rectify inaccurate data;
  • Request erasure ("right to be forgotten");
  • Restrict processing;
  • Data portability where applicable;
  • Object to processing based on legitimate interest;
  • Lodge a complaint with the competent supervisory authority (President of the Personal Data Protection Office in Poland).

7. Data Security

The Foundation implements appropriate technical and organizational measures to ensure confidentiality, integrity, and availability of personal data.

Security practices include access control, encryption where appropriate, audit trails, and risk assessment procedures.

8. AI Systems and Data Processing

Where digital systems, including analytical or AI-based tools, are used in Foundation projects, processing is conducted in compliance with GDPR principles of transparency, data minimization, and accountability.

Where required, Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIA) and risk assessments are performed.

9. International Transfers

If personal data is transferred outside the European Economic Area, appropriate safeguards are implemented in accordance with Chapter V of the GDPR.

10. Updates

This Privacy Policy may be updated to reflect changes in legal requirements or operational practices. The current version is published on this website.
