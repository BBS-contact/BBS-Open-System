Statute (Public Extract – Aligned Version)

Informational summary consistent with the registered Polish-language Statute

1. Legal Basis and Supervision

Fundacja BBS — Better Balance System ("Foundation") operates pursuant to the Act of 6 April 1984 on Foundations, the Act of 24 April 2003 on Public Benefit and Volunteer Work, and its registered Statute.

The Foundation is entered into the National Court Register (KRS) under number 0001220296 and has its registered seat in Brodnica, Poland.

Supervision over the Foundation is exercised by the minister competent for digital affairs (Minister Cyfryzacji), in accordance with applicable Polish law.

2. Duration and Territorial Scope

The Foundation is established for an indefinite period.

The territorial scope of activities covers the Republic of Poland and, where necessary for the proper realization of its objectives, also activities abroad.

3. Statutory Objectives

The Foundation conducts socially beneficial activities in particular in the following areas:

  • Support for digitalization of public administration and implementation of transparent management systems (audit-trail frameworks);
  • Development and promotion of ethical digital technologies and artificial intelligence in the public interest;
  • Education, digital literacy, prevention of digital exclusion and disinformation, and open knowledge platforms;
  • Science, higher education, research and development (R&D) in technical and social sciences, including AI systems, data models, tools, and digital infrastructure;
  • Health protection, cybermedicine, and technologies supporting citizen well-being;
  • Support for civil society, public participation, transparency, and open access to information;
  • Human rights protection, non-discrimination, and anti-corruption initiatives;
  • European integration, international cooperation, sustainable development, and environmental protection;
  • Information security, privacy, GDPR compliance, data ethics, and AI safety.

The Foundation’s objectives are interpreted broadly as activities serving the common good, including those implemented through digital means.

4. Forms of Activity and PKD Classification

The Foundation pursues its objectives through unpaid and paid public benefit activities and may conduct business activities exclusively to the extent serving its statutory purposes.

The principal PKD classifications include:

  • 94.99.Z – Activities of other membership organizations (predominant);
  • 62.01.Z – Software-related activities;
  • 62.02.Z – IT consultancy;
  • 62.09.Z – Other IT services;
  • 63.11.Z – Data processing and hosting activities;
  • 85.60.Z – Educational support activities;
  • 72.19.Z – Research and experimental development.

All income derived from economic activity is allocated exclusively to statutory purposes.

5. Assets and Financial Governance

The founding capital amounts to PLN 2,000.00, allocated between statutory and economic activity in accordance with the registered Statute.

The Foundation’s assets may originate from donations, grants (including EU funds), subsidies, inheritances, paid public benefit activities, economic activity, and other lawful sources.

The Foundation maintains accounting records in compliance with Polish accounting regulations and prepares annual financial and substantive reports, which are published in accordance with legal requirements.

6. Governance Structure

The governing bodies of the Foundation are the Management Board (Zarząd) and, optionally, a Supervisory Council (Rada Fundacji).

The Management Board consists of one to three members. The President of the Management Board is appointed in accordance with the registered Statute.

The Founder retains personal rights defined in the Statute, including rights related to appointment of board members, amendment of the Statute, and decision on liquidation.

The President of the Management Board is authorized to represent the Foundation independently.

7. Intellectual Property and LEO System

Proprietary and personal copyrights to the LEO system (including source code, algorithms, and documentation) remain the exclusive property of the Founder.

The Foundation is granted a non-exclusive, revocable license to use the LEO system solely for the realization of its statutory objectives.

The system may not be used by third parties for independent commercial enrichment.

8. Ethics, Privacy and AI Governance

The Foundation develops and maintains digital systems in compliance with GDPR and recognized information security standards.

Where required by law, the Foundation conducts Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIA) and AI risk assessments and maintains transparency documentation.

Core principles include transparency, non-discrimination, protection of human rights, algorithmic openness (within legal limits), and personal data protection.

9. Amendment and Liquidation

Amendments to the Statute and decisions regarding liquidation are carried out in accordance with the procedures defined in the registered Statute.

In the event of liquidation, remaining assets are allocated to objectives consistent with the statutory mission.
