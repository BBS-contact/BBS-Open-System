## 1. Purpose of This Document

This Position Paper formally defines the institutional scope, boundaries, and operational intent of the LEO (Logical Epistemic Oversight) system.

It serves as an official reference for:

* academic institutions,
* public bodies,
* regulatory authorities,
* research partners,
* technical collaborators,
* auditors and compliance reviewers.

This document clarifies what LEO is, what it is not, and under which institutional principles it operates.

## 3. Core Definition

LEO (Logical Epistemic Oversight) is:

> A structured governance-support architecture designed to enhance traceability, documentation integrity, and institutional decision-support processes.

LEO operates as:

* an advisory system,
* a structured documentation integrity layer,
* a research-driven governance modeling framework.

LEO does not replace institutional authority and does not issue legally binding decisions.

## 4. Functional Scope

LEO is designed to:

* improve documentation traceability,
* support structured decision processes,
* provide cryptographic integrity anchoring mechanisms,
* enable audit-friendly record management,
* model compliance-aware AI-assisted workflows.

LEO integrates:

* modular architecture,
* human oversight integration,
* structured logging,
* integrity verification mechanisms,
* governance-aligned system boundaries.

## 5. Integrity Architecture Principles

The system is based on the following integrity principles:

1. Immutable state transitions (append-only logic).
2. Cryptographic state anchoring (Merkle-based integrity layer).
3. Digital signature validation for freeze states.
4. Human-supervised governance enforcement.
5. Clear separation between advisory output and legal authority.

No absolute guarantee of invulnerability is asserted.
LEO is designed as a structured mitigation and traceability enhancement framework.
