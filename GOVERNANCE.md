# Project Governance

The LEO research project is developed within the institutional framework of:

**Fundacja BBS – Better Balance System**

Registered non-profit foundation  
Poland · European Union

---

# Governance Model

The project follows a research-driven governance model designed to ensure:

- institutional accountability
- transparent development processes
- responsible AI and governance practices

Strategic direction of the project is coordinated by the BBS Foundation.

---

# Decision Process

Development decisions are based on:

- research objectives
- system integrity considerations
- regulatory compliance
- institutional collaboration opportunities

Major architectural decisions are documented within the repository.

---

# Institutional Oversight

The LEO system is developed as a research infrastructure intended to support:

- institutional transparency
- anomaly detection research
- governance analysis tools
- advisory decision-support systems

The system does not replace institutional authority and does not issue legally binding decisions.

---

# Development Principles

The project follows several core principles:

- modular system architecture
- transparency of research documentation
- responsible AI practices
- regulatory awareness
- institutional collaboration readiness

---

# Repository Role

The repository serves as a public research documentation environment containing:

- architectural models
- prototype demonstrations
- governance documentation
- research artifacts

Core runtime components remain part of the protected intellectual property of the BBS Foundation.
